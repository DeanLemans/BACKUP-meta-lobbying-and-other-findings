[RECIPIENT NAME],

I'm sending this because [you covered the Targeted Victory campaign in 2022 / your organization has been tracking age verification legislation / you've reported on Meta's lobbying operations] and I have documentation on what appears to be a continuation of the same playbook.

I've spent several months pulling public records on the age verification bills moving through US state legislatures. The findings are published with full sources here: https://github.com/upper-up/meta-lobbying-and-other-findings

Here's what the records show.

**Meta wrote one of the bills.** Rep. Kim Carver (R-Bossier City), the sponsor of Louisiana HB-570, publicly confirmed a Meta lobbyist brought the legislative language directly to her. The bill requires app stores to verify ages. It does not require social media platforms to do anything. Meta deployed 12 lobbyists across 9 firms for this single bill, paying at least $324,992. The bill passed 99-0. The lobbyists were there to control the text, not the votes.

**The bills cover all operating systems with no open-source exemption.** California AB-1043 (effective Jan 2027) defines "Operating System Provider" as anyone who "develops, licenses, or controls" an OS on any general-purpose computing device. That includes volunteer-maintained Linux distributions. The EU's equivalent framework explicitly exempts FOSS. Six US states have enacted or introduced identical legislation from the same ICMEC template. None contain a FOSS exemption.

**Meta's LD-2 filings name the bill.** Meta's own Senate lobbying disclosures explicitly list H.R. 3149/S. 1586, the App Store Accountability Act, as a lobbied bill. In the same filing, Meta lobbies against KOSA and COPPA 2.0, which would regulate Meta directly. It supports legislation that burdens Apple and Google and fights legislation that burdens itself.

**The advocacy group pushing these bills doesn't legally exist.** The Digital Childhood Alliance, which testifies in favor of ASAA bills across states, has no EIN in the IRS Business Master File and no incorporation record in any state registry I searched. Bloomberg exposed Meta as a funder in July 2025. DCA's executive director admitted receiving tech company funding under oath at a Louisiana Senate hearing but refused to name donors.

**One firm bridges the super PAC and the astroturf.** Hilltop Public Solutions co-leads Meta's $45M ATEP super PAC and coordinates DCA's messaging. This is the first confirmed entity connecting Meta's political spending to DCA's advocacy campaign.

**I checked the dark money pathway.** I parsed $2.0 billion in IRS Schedule I grants across all five Arabella Advisors entities (4,433 grants). Zero dollars went to any child safety or tech policy organization. The grant pathway is ruled out. Meta's Colorado lobbyist (Adam Eichberg, Headwaters Strategies, $338,500 from Meta) simultaneously chairs the board of the New Venture Fund, which transfers $121.3M annually to a 501(c)(4) with no donor disclosure. The structural overlap is documented even though the grant pathway is excluded.

Every finding in the repo cites a primary source: Senate LD-2 filings, IRS 990s, state lobbying registrations, campaign finance databases, corporate registries, and Wayback Machine archives. I used Claude Code as a research tool for bulk data parsing; the methodology disclosure is in the repo.

I'm not pitching a story. I'm sending you the records and letting you decide what to do with them. If any of this is useful, I'm available to walk through the sourcing.
